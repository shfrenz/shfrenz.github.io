About Me

"Knowledge alone is capable of transforming the world, while at the same time leaving it exactly as it is. When you look at the world with knowledge, you realize that things are unchangeable and at the same time are constantly being transformed."

Hi! Welcome to my personal website. This is Shoufeng Zhang, an incoming freshman at Georgia Institute of Technology, majoring in computer engineering. I am an aspiring engineer passionate about computer architecture, digital systems, and machine learning. My recent work includes FPGA-based hardware design, systolic array acceleration for quantized neural networks, and system-level simulations using Verilog and PyRTL.
I am deeply curious about how low-level systems design can reshape the efficiency and accessibility of AI computing, and I'm eager to explore areas like AI/ML systems, embedded design, and scalable infrastructure. Currently, I'm learning more about operating systems, architectures, and hardware-aware machine learning algorithms.

Selected Projects

Hardware-Accelerated 1-Bit Quantization Using PyRTL for Efficient Neural Network Design

Mentored by a PhD from Georgia Tech, I designed a 1-bit multiply-accumulate (MAC) unit for a Systolic Array, handling the weights and input ternary properties of XNOR-Net. This work significantly cut down the area requirement and the critical path length.

Yishengyizhen Program by Chinese Academy of Sciences

In this program, I delved into computer architecture and systems, learning Digital Logic and Computer Organization. In the basic stage, I applied Verilog and Vivado to design and simulate a circuit capable of executing RV32 instructions, and implemented a simplified RV32I emulator in C.
