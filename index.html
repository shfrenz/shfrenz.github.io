About Me

"Knowledge alone is capable of transforming the world, while at the same time leaving it exactly as it is. When you look at the world with knowledge, you realize that things are unchangeable and at the same time are constantly being transformed."

Hi! Welcome to my personal website. This is Shoufeng Zhang (张寿峰 in Chinese). My academic interests lie at the intersection of computer architecture, digital system design, and machine learning systems. In recent years, I have explored areas such as RISC-V-based CPU design, FPGA system prototyping, low-bit quantized neural network accelerators, and hardware-aware machine learning optimization.
For my future academics exploration, I am particularly interested in exploring AI/ML system co-design, including how emerging hardware can shape the next generation of machine learning algorithms, and how system-level optimization can unlock efficiency for real-world AI workloads. I am also curious about robot learning and embodied intelligence, aiming to bridge low-level system control with high-level decision-making to create intelligent and adaptable agents for complex tasks. Fields like operating systems, compilers, and ML-specific hardware accelerators are on my learning roadmap as I seek to build a comprehensive understanding of both hardware and software layers.
I am always open to conversations, collaboration opportunities, and creative projects — feel free to reach out if you share similar passions! (zhangshoufeng07@gmail.com)

Selected Projects

Hardware-Accelerated 1-Bit Quantization Using PyRTL for Efficient Neural Network Design

Mentored by Mr. James Read from Georgia Tech, I designed a 1-bit multiply-accumulate (MAC) unit for a Systolic Array with PyRTL, handling the weights and input ternary properties of XNOR-Net. This work significantly cut down the area requirement and the critical path length.

Yishengyizhen Program by Chinese Academy of Sciences

In ysyx program, I delved into computer architecture and systems, learning Digital Logic and Computer Organization. In the basic stage, I applied Verilog and Vivado to design and simulate a circuit capable of executing RV32 instructions, and implemented a simplified RV32I emulator in C, including features like memory access, ALU operations, and trace functions.

A Comprehensive Analysis of AI Research Topics Using LDA and LDAvis: Insights from arXiv Data

Mentored by Prof. Gangyi Ding from Beijing Institute of Technology during the Science Talent Program. This project presents an in-depth analysis of AI research topics using Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) and LDAvis, leveraging data from arXiv. The PDF contains the full report and findings.

International Mathematical Modelling Challenge (IMMC) 2024 Report

The official report submitted for the 2024 International Mathematical Modelling Challenge, showcasing the finalist-level strategy and results on modelling and reducing CO2 emissions.
